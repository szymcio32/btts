## What Went Well

1. **Safety chain delivered end-to-end** -- Epic 4 now covers pre-kickoff consolidation, game-start recovery, and post-recovery verification as one coherent protective flow for filled positions.
2. **Strong reliability posture in critical path** -- Story 4.3 closed the major gap left by initial game-start re-placement by adding verify-and-retry behavior until a sell is confirmed active.
3. **Concurrency risk handled explicitly** -- Story 4.2 introduced locking and atomic sell recording, which directly reduced race conditions in concurrent recovery scenarios.
4. **Design continuity across stories** -- Story 4.1's buy-price sell policy and service boundaries were preserved through Stories 4.2 and 4.3 without architectural drift.
5. **Quality scaled with complexity** -- tests expanded materially across all three stories, with no reported regressions in story records.

---

## Challenges and Friction

1. **Process-state mismatch still present** -- all Epic 4 stories are `done`, but `sprint-status.yaml` still shows `epic-4: in-progress`; this repeats a known artifact drift pattern.
2. **Cross-file ripple from constructor changes** -- service dependency changes required broad updates in wiring and tests, increasing implementation coordination overhead.
3. **Safety logic complexity increased sharply** -- recovery now spans scheduling, lifecycle transitions, threaded execution, API retries, and verification loops; this raises maintenance burden.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Recovery is a chain, not a single event** -- cancellation detection without verification is incomplete for safety-critical trading behavior.
2. **Thread safety is foundational, not optional** -- once per-game recovery threads were introduced, locking and atomic operations became mandatory prerequisites.
3. **State-machine signaling worked well** -- leaving failed pre-kickoff games in non-advanced states provided a practical implicit handoff into game-start recovery.
4. **Process debt recurs when manual** -- status drift continues to recur despite prior retrospectives, confirming automation is the right lever.
